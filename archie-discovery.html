🔴 Priority 1 — Act Now
12 signals Output Reliability & Consistency
What users experience

Lawyers run the same prompt twice and get completely different results. Archie summarises 30 memos correctly one day, then randomly skips half of them the next. A paralegal analysing a property matter finds Archie missed the key properties entirely. Users don't know if an output is accurate until they manually check it — which defeats the whole purpose.

Why it blocks growth

In legal work, a wrong output isn't just unhelpful — it's a professional liability. When users can't predict Archie's reliability, they use it less, recommend it less, and fall back to manual work. This is the primary ceiling on daily active usage growing from 4K to 8K. No amount of new features fixes this.

Evidence
  • Same prompt randomly selects which memos to include — paralegals re-run 3+ times within minutes hoping for an acceptable result ↗ Twine Feb 2
  • Knowledge base returns inaccurate, off-base answers — one customer stopped using it entirely after repeated failures ↗ Twine Feb 2
  • Archie fails to identify properties listed in a matter brief — Burch Shepard Family Law (Feb 16) ↗ Twine Feb 16
  • Time constraint instructions in prompts are ignored — a firm specifying date ranges for file reviews gets results that ignore the timeframe ↗ Twine Feb 9
  • Co-Counsel is preferred for complex bank statement analysis in high-asset divorce — customer routes hard analytical work to Co-Counsel because Archie is less reliable for it ↗ Twine Feb 23
  • AI model versions feel outdated — ChatGPT 4.0 perceived as old, customer notes Gemini 3 is out while Archie still shows Gemini 2.5 ↗ Twine Feb 9
Action: Invest   Build evals pipeline to catch regressions · fix memo chunking randomness · add inline citations to outputs · define model version upgrade cadence
8 signals Bugs & Stability
What users experience

Mid-task, things break. The chat history panel disappears without warning and the user is back at a blank screen. They switched the AI model to Gemini last week but it's back to default today. The Archie tab just won't load. These aren't setup issues — they're happening to active users in the middle of real legal work.

Why it blocks growth

Bugs in the core workflow destroy trust in what already works, which is worse than a missing feature. Chat history loss is like losing a Word document mid-draft — it's visceral. These failures hit the users with the highest retention value: people who are already invested and using Archie daily.

Evidence
  • Chat history flashes and disappears when clicking past conversations — user is reset to a blank state with no recovery path; reported across multiple firms ↗ Twine Feb 2 ↗ Slack #archie-escalations
  • AI model preference resets to default after logout — user sets Claude or Gemini, comes back next session and it's gone ↗ Twine Feb 12
  • Archie tab fails to load — user must do a full page refresh to get it back ↗ Twine Feb 12
  • File ingestion counter shows the wrong number after upload — creates confusion about what Archie has actually read ↗ Slack #archie-escalations (BrotmanLaw, Mar 5)
  • Archie freezes or stops responding mid-task with no loading indicator or recovery path shown
Action: Fix P1   Chat history persistence = P1 · model preference persistence = P1 · tab load failure = P1 · instrument real-user monitoring to detect before users escalate
9 signals Data Access & Context Gaps
What users experience

Archie is sold as a Smokeball-native AI, but it can't see most of what's already in Smokeball. Contact cards, internal memos, email attachments, precedent templates — all invisible to Archie. Users have to download files and re-upload them, copy-paste client details manually, or switch to a different tool entirely. The product asks users to bring the context it should already have.

Why it blocks growth

The core reason to choose Archie over generic AI tools like ChatGPT is that it knows your matters. When it doesn't, the value proposition collapses. Every data gap is a reason to open a different tool instead. These gaps also hurt in demos — prospects see what Archie can't access and underestimate its value.

Evidence
  • Archie can't read contact cards inside a matter — users manually copy-paste client details into the chat every time ↗ Twine Feb 2
  • Archie can't access internal memos or precedent templates saved in a matter — lawyers re-upload their own templates each time they need Archie to use them ↗ Twine Feb 2
  • Archie can't read email attachments — users must download every attachment and re-upload it before Archie can analyse it ↗ Twine Feb 9
  • No Eve AI integration — a prospect uses Eve specifically for discovery responses; moving to Smokeball means abandoning the tool they rely on ↗ Twine Mar 2
  • Archie is only accessible from the matter screen — not from within Word, where most drafting actually happens (integration described as "coming") ↗ Twine Feb 9
  • Standalone Claude + Midpage seen as more capable for legal research — because it connects to real case law databases that Archie doesn't access ↗ Twine Mar 2
Action: Phase it   Contact cards + memo access via Agents context retrieval (in current scope) · confirm Word panel delivery date · external legal research = strategic decision needed
8 signals Usability, Discoverability & Onboarding
What users experience

Users either don't know Archie can do something, or they know it exists but can't figure out how. A firm manually reviewed contracts for months without knowing Archie could do it. Another user saved a prompt and couldn't find it again. Prospects ask "what's the difference between Archie and Lawy?" mid-demo and don't get a clear answer from the product. Capabilities that exist go unused.

Why it blocks growth

Every feature that isn't discovered has zero impact. Discoverability gaps compound every other investment — improving the bank statement analyser doesn't help if users don't know it exists. This is also an active sales problem: prospects judge Archie's capability by what they can intuitively find during a demo, not by the full feature list.

Evidence
  • Prompt library is confusing — users can't find prompts they themselves saved, because prompts are scoped by matter type but the product doesn't make this clear ↗ Twine Feb 9 ↗ Twine Feb 16
  • Archie vs Lawy confusion — during demos, prospects ask "what's the difference between Archie and Lawy?" and "where do we find that?"; the distinction isn't evident from the UI ↗ Twine Jan 26
  • Contract review capability is unknown to active customers — a firm was manually highlighting and generating letters for months before learning Archie could do it ↗ Twine Feb 23
  • Document analysis capability doesn't sell itself — it needs a live demo to land; prospects can't infer what Archie can do from browsing the interface ↗ Twine Feb 16
  • Prompts disappear when switching matter types — users don't know their prompts are matter-type scoped, so they think their saved work is gone ↗ Twine Feb 9
Action: Quick wins   Clarify Archie vs Lawy in-product · build matter-type starter prompts · make prompt library searchable · proactively surface contract review to existing users
7 signals Competitive Perception Gap
What users experience

When prospects and customers compare Archie to other AI tools, Archie often loses the comparison — even in areas where it is objectively strong. The gap is concentrated in two specific areas: complex analytical tasks like bank statement analysis for family law, and legal research, where competitors connect directly to case law databases. At the same time, Archie's genuine advantages (pricing, Leap comparison, Clio reliability) frequently don't come up in evaluations.

Why it blocks growth

Perception shapes purchase decisions. Even if Archie's output quality improves, it won't win deals if evaluators believe a gap exists that doesn't. The good news: the competitive loss to Co-Counsel is specific and addressable — it's about a narrow task type, not a broad AI quality failure. And Archie has real competitive wins that just aren't landing.

Evidence
  • Co-Counsel described as "probably a smarter model" — a customer uses both, defaulting to Co-Counsel for bank statement analysis in high-asset divorce cases where precision is critical ↗ Twine Feb 23
  • Prospects view Archie as inferior to Clio for AI capabilities during product evaluations ↗ Twine Feb 23
  • No built-in legal research database — Archie requires manual case uploads; competitors with Midpage or Lexis integration have case law at hand without the extra step ↗ Twine Mar 2
  • ✅ Archie wins on pricing — included with Smokeball at no extra cost; Co-Counsel and Clio charge per-use fees on top of the platform subscription ↗ Twine Mar 2
  • ✅ Archie wins vs Leap — Leap's AI is described as "very basic"; Archie has knowledge sources that Leap doesn't offer at all ↗ Twine Mar 2 ↗ Twine Feb 11
  • ✅ Archie wins vs Clio's reliability — existing Clio customers describe its AI as producing unreliable outputs; Archie is seen as more dependable by comparison ↗ Twine Jan 26
Action: Targeted   Improve bank statement analysis accuracy · evaluate legal research gap (own vs. partner) · equip sales with clear competitive talking points for pricing and Leap/Clio comparisons

💡 Key Insights
1
Reliability is the ceiling, not a floor. Every week of Twine data shows consistency as the top pain cluster. Users don't churn because Archie is missing a feature — they churn because they can't predict when it will work. Fix reliability and every other improvement compounds.
2
Archie's data blindness undermines its own positioning. A Smokeball-native AI that can't see contact cards, memos, or email attachments forces users to manually bridge the gap — defeating the core reason to use it over ChatGPT. Context access is foundational, not a nice-to-have.
3
Bugs are hitting the most valuable users. Chat history loss, model preference reset, and tab failures affect people already invested in Archie — daily users, not new ones. These are retention risks, not activation risks.
4
Features that users don't discover don't exist. Contract review capability, audio summarisation, bank statement analysis — all praised when users find them, all invisible to many who need them. Discoverability is currently costing real deals and real retention.
5
The competitive loss to Co-Counsel is specific and winnable. It's about one task type: complex document analysis in high-asset family law. This is not a broad AI capability failure — it's a targeted accuracy gap that can be attacked directly.
6
Archie's real strengths aren't landing in evaluations. Pricing advantage, Leap comparison, knowledge sources, and Clio reliability superiority are all documented wins — but they're not surfacing when it matters. The product is better than evaluators believe.
7
Growing enthusiasm for audio transcription may just need a discoverability fix. Three consecutive weeks of unprompted excitement for a feature that already exists. Before building more, verify whether users are being shown it at all.

🧪 Validation Experiments
E1
Warning Banner Trust Test Does showing a limitation warning increase or decrease trust? · Week 1 · S effort
▼

Hypothesis

≥80% of lawyers who see a partial-analysis warning will say it makes them more confident in Archie's outputs, not less.

Setup

  • Two Figma prototypes: (A) current response with no warning, (B) same response with banner "⚠️ Only 34 of 87 pages were analysed"
  • Recruit 5–8 lawyers from current Archie users (AU/US mix)
  • Moderated 20-min sessions — show both variants, ask "How does this affect your trust?"

Decision Criteria

  • ≥4/5 prefer the warning → ship the banner, proceed to E5
  • <3/5 prefer it → reframe as positive source indicator, retest before building
E2
Eval Pipeline Calibration Study Can automated scoring correlate with lawyer quality perception? · Week 2–3 · M effort
▼

Hypothesis

A judge-model eval harness can score Archie's legal outputs with Spearman ρ > 0.65 vs. lawyer ratings.

Setup

  • Sample 50 recent Archie responses from non-confidential test matters
  • 2 lawyers independently rate each on 1–5: factual accuracy, completeness, relevance
  • Run same 50 through Claude Opus with a legal accuracy rubric
  • Calculate Spearman correlation

Decision Criteria

  • ρ > 0.65 → build full pipeline with daily sampling
  • ρ 0.4–0.65 → iterate rubric before scaling
  • ρ < 0.4 → use regeneration rate + copy-rate as quality proxies instead
E3
Contextual Prompt Suggestions A/B Test Will users click matter-aware prompts? · Week 1–2 · S–M effort
▼

Hypothesis

>25% of new users who see matter-aware prompt suggestions will click one in their first session (vs. <10% from blank box).
